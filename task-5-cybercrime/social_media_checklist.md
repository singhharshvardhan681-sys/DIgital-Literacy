# Stay Safe Online Checklist for College Students in India

## Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist

1. **Use strong and unique passwords**  
   Create passwords with a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using your name, birth date, or simple passwords

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)**  
   Turn on two-factor authentication for email, social media, banking apps, and academic portals to add an extra layer of security.

3. **Do not click unknown links or attachments**  
   Avoid opening suspicious links received through email, SMS, or social media, especially if they ask for urgent action or personal details.

4. **Keep devices and apps updated**  
   Regularly update your phone, laptop, browser, and apps to protect against newly discovered security threats.

5. **Use secure Wi-Fi networks**  
   Avoid logging into banking apps or entering passwords while connected to public Wi-Fi in cafes, libraries, or railway stations.

6. **Verify online shopping and job offers**  
   Check website authenticity before making payments or sharing documents. Fake internships, scholarships, and shopping websites often target students.

7. **Never share OTP, UPI PIN, or bank details**  
   No bank, payment app, or official service will ask for your OTP or UPI PIN through calls or messages.

8. **Check UPI payment requests carefully**  
   In UPI apps, remember that entering your PIN means **sending money**, not receiving it. Reject unknown collect requests.

9. **Confirm QR codes before scanning**  
   Fraudsters may send fake QR codes for refunds or offers. Scanning a QR code can lead to unauthorized payment.

10. **Review privacy settings on social media**  
   Limit who can see your personal information, photos, phone number, and location.

11. **Log out from shared devices**  
   Always sign out from email, banking, and social media accounts on public or college computers.

12. **Report cyber fraud immediately**  
   If you face cyber fraud, report it quickly through:

   - **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in  
   - **Cyber Crime Helpline:** **1930**


Being alert online helps prevent identity theft, financial fraud, and misuse of personal information. Small precautions taken daily can protect your digital life.
